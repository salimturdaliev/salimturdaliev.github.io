Research

Evidence, mechanisms, and policy implications

My work examines how energy and climate policies affect household behavior and welfare. These briefs translate selected studies into accessible accounts of the research question, evidence, findings, and policy lessons.

Behavioral intervention / Armenia

Nudging energy conservation in Armenia

Households receiving social-comparison reports used less electricity. A long pause showed that part of the effect persisted, while restarting the reports restored their original impact.

Energy pricing / Russia

Russia's block tariff experiment

Increasing block tariffs produced modest electricity savings while encouraging some vulnerable households to switch toward dirtier fuels, revealing important distributional trade-offs.

Energy insecurity / Kyrgyzstan

Electricity outages and child growth

Frequent outages were associated with worse height-for-age outcomes among young children, showing why electricity reliability is a health and development issue.
